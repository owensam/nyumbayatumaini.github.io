Our Story of Hope

From a humble feeding program to a sanctuary of transformation, our journey is fueled by the belief that every child deserves a future.

From Humble Beginnings

Nyumba ya Tumaini was founded by Ben Muya in 2002 under the patronage of the late Pastor Charles Baraza. It began as a feeding program at Shauri Moyo Baptist Church, providing food, clothing, and hygiene care to over 300 street-connected children and youth.

A Necessary Evolution

In 2003, the program relocated to Ongata Rongai to become a residential center, addressing the critical need for a safe haven. For over 18 years, we have transformed the lives of over 500 children, reuniting many with their families and equipping others with vocational skills in mechanics, carpentry, and masonry, empowering them to build brighter, sustainable futures.

Our Core Belief

We believe that our Youth are the future of Kenya. With increased community involvement and sponsor support, we aim to build a facility with critical resources and sustainable revenue opportunities for continued growth of our programs.

Rescue: A Safe Beginning

We remove boys from the immediate dangers of street life, ensuring their fundamental rights are protected. This initial step provides a sanctuary with temporal care, security, nourishment, and all other basic needs, creating a stable foundation for their journey of healing and growth.

Rehabilitate: Healing from Within

Rehabilitation is a journey of healing the mind, body, and spirit. We provide our rescued children with spiritual guidance, one-on-one counseling, and comprehensive psychosocial support. This is complemented by literacy classes, diverse sporting activities, dedicated mentorship, and life skills training, all supported by essential medical care.

Reintegrate: Rebuilding Family Bonds

The ultimate goal for many is to return home. Our team facilitates meticulous home tracing to reconnect rehabilitated boys with their families and communities. For children who require long-term alternative care, we provide unwavering support and a loving home at NYT, ensuring they are never alone.

Re-Socialize: Thriving in Community

Sustainable change happens within a supportive community. We focus on the economic empowerment of reintegrated families to help them stay together. We connect boys to community programs, cultural events, formal education, vocational training, church life, recreation, and health centers. We provide ongoing primary, secondary, and tertiary education support to ensure they have the tools to build their own futures.
